# Learning Path: "Minimum DS + Maximum Practical"

## Why this lesson

See how the course is sequenced so you can budget time and avoid skipping foundations needed for later projects.

## Outcomes

-  Understand the phased roadmap (Foundation  ML Engineering  Modern AI  Projects  Career)
-  Know what "Minimum DS, Maximum Practical" means in practice
-  Have a weekly schedule template to follow

**Time**: ~15 minutes

**Prerequisites**: None

---

##  The Problem with Traditional AI Courses

I've taken dozens of AI courses. Most of them follow the same frustrating pattern:

1.  **Week 1-4**: Linear Algebra and Calculus.
2.  **Week 5-8**: Probability and Statistics.
3.  **Week 9**: Finally, you write some code... but it's in a messy notebook that only works on your laptop.

**The Result**: You finish the course knowing the math behind a Support Vector Machine, but you have no idea how to deploy a model to production or how to build a RAG system.

You become a **Researcher**, not an **Engineer**.

---

##  Our Philosophy: "Minimum DS + Maximum Practical"

This course flips that model upside down.

We believe that **you don't need a PhD to build AI systems**. You need engineering skills.

### What "Minimum Data Science" Means
We will teach you *just enough* theory to understand what you are doing, but we won't get bogged down in proofs.

-   **We WILL teach**: What overfitting is, how to spot it, and how to fix it.
-   **We WON'T teach**: The mathematical derivation of the bias-variance tradeoff.
-   **We WILL teach**: How embeddings represent meaning.
-   **We WON'T teach**: The matrix factorization math behind Word2Vec.

### What "Maximum Practical" Means
Every concept we introduce is immediately followed by code.

-   **Concept**: "Models need to be saved."
-   **Code**: We write a function to pickle a model.
-   **Production**: We wrap that model in a Docker container.

##  The Roadmap

Here is the journey we are going to take together.

### Phase 1: The Foundation (Weeks 1-2)
*Goal: Get comfortable with the tools.*
-   **Python for AI**: Advanced Python features (decorators, type hints) that libraries use.
-   **Data Science Basics**: Pandas and NumPy for data manipulation.
-   **Mini-Lab**: Cleaning a dirty dataset.

### Phase 2: ML Engineering (Weeks 3-4)
*Goal: Build and serve a model.*
-   **ML Basics**: Training a model with Scikit-Learn and XGBoost.
-   **Model Serving**: Building a FastAPI wrapper.
-   **Containers**: Dockerizing your API.
-   **Mini-Project**: Deploying a churn predictor.

### Phase 3: Modern AI (Weeks 5-6)
*Goal: Understand LLMs.*
-   **LLM Fundamentals**: Tokens, context windows, temperature.
-   **Embeddings**: Vector databases and semantic search.
-   **RAG**: Building a retrieval pipeline.
-   **Mini-Lab**: Building a "Chat with PDF" tool.

### Phase 4: The Build Phase (Weeks 7-12)
*Goal: Build your portfolio.*
-   **Project 1**: MLOps Churn System (End-to-End).
-   **Project 2**: RAG Knowledge Assistant (Production RAG).
-   **Project 3**: DevOps Agent (Autonomous Agents).

### Phase 5: Career Prep (Week 13)
*Goal: Get hired.*
-   **Resume Review**: How to position your new skills.
-   **System Design**: How to ace the "Design a RAG system" interview question.

##  Suggested Schedule

This course is self-paced, but consistency is key.

**If you have 5 hours/week:**
-   Focus on one lesson per week.
-   Complete the labs on weekends.
-   *Duration: ~20 weeks.*

**If you have 10-15 hours/week (Recommended):**
-   Complete one section per week.
-   Spend significant time on the portfolio projects.
-   *Duration: ~10-12 weeks.*
